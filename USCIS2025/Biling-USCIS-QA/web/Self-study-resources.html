Questions 100-118: Recent American History and Other Important Historical Information

Q100 "Name one war fought by the United States in the 1900s.": Study Guide p.63

Q101 "Why did the United States enter World War I?": Study Guide p.63

Note: the Study Guide text does not explicitly state a reason why the U.S. entered World War I. Acceptable answers are: "because Germany attacked U.S. (civilian) ships", "to support the Allied Powers (England, France, Italy, and Russia)" and "to oppose the Central Powers (Germany, Austria-Hungary, the Ottoman Empire, and Bulgaria)".

Q102 "When did all women get the right to vote?": Study Guide p.59

Note: the Study Guide does not mention the 19th amendment by name. Acceptable answers are: "1920", "after World War I", or "19th Amendment".

Q108 "Who was the United States’ main rival during the Cold War?": Study Guide p.65

Note: Although the words "main rival" are not used in the Study Guide, "Russia", "USSR" or "the Soviet Union "are acceptable answers to question 108.

Q109 "During the Cold War, what was one main concern of the United States?": Study Guide p.65

Q110 "Why did the United States enter the Korean War?": Study Guide p.65

Q111 "Why did the United States enter the Vietnam War?": Study Guide p.65

Note: for questions 110 and 111, the reason provided in the Study Guide text is "Other countries like China and North Korea were also becoming communist. The U.S. wanted to stop other countries around the world from becoming communist." By implication Vietnam is included, despite not being mentioned in this page. An accpetable answer is "to stop the spread of communism".

Q112 "What did the civil rights movement do?": Study Guide p.66

Q113 "Martin Luther King, Jr. is famous for many things. Name one."*: Study Guide p.66

Q114 "Why did the United States enter the Persian Gulf War?": Study Guide p.63

Note: the Study Guide text does not provide any reason for U.S. entering the (Persian) Gulf War. An acceptable answer is "to force the Iraqi military from Kuwait".

Q115 "What major event happened on September 11, 2001 in the United States?"*: Study Guide p.67

Q116 "Name one U.S. military conflict after the September 11, 2001 attacks.": Study Guide p.67

Note: despite the mention of events after 9/11/2001, the wars fought by the U.S. in the Middle East and Afghanistan are not mentioned in the text of the Study Guide. Acceptable answers are: "War on Terror", "war in Afghanistan" and "war in Iraq".

Q117 "Name one American Indian tribe in the United States. For a complete list of tribes, please visit bia.gov.": Study Guide p.55

Q118 "Name one example of an American innovation. Study Guide p.65

Note: the Study Guide does not use the word "innovation" but there is a sentence "In 1969, the U.S. became the first country to land a person on the moon." Other acceptable answers which do not occur in the Study Guide are: "light bulb", "automobile (cars, internal combustion en-gine)", "skyscrapers", "airplane", "assembly line", and "integrated circuit".
